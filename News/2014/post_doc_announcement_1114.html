Postdoctoral Researcher Position in Computer Systems at Purdue University

Posting date: November 23, 2014

The Dependable Computing Systems Lab (DCSL) in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Purdue University, is inviting applications for a postdoctoral researcher position in practical distributed computer systems. We are looking for candidates with strong computer systems building skills. In this exciting position, the researcher will get to work with a group of excellent graduate and undergraduate students, diverse faculty from both ECE and CS, and present his/her work in front of top people from academia, funding agencies, and industry.

 

When does it start? From January 15, 2015.

Where is it? West Lafayette, IN

How long is it? 1 year, with the possibility of extension depending on renewal of funding and satisfactory progress

Compensation? Very competitive

Requirements

Applicants should

  • have a Ph.D. in CS or ECE from a top US or Canadian department and have publications in top-tier conferences.
  • possess good systems building skills.
  • possess some experience with data analysis.
  • have excellent communication skills.
  • be creative and have leadership qualities.

Context

The Dependable Computing Systems Lab (DCSL) is part of Purdue University's School of Electrical and Computer Engineering. It is one of the laboratories that is a part of the university-wide security center called CERIAS. DCSL has a stellar group of researchers (approximately 15 currently), excellent computational facilities, relaxed and flexible work environment, and a collegial collaborative environment with multiple faculty at Purdue and other schools and industrial collaborators.

Prospective candidates are requested to send an email containing a 1-page research statement, a CV, and names of 3 references. Application screening will start immediately and continue until the position is filled.
