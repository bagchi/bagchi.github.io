Announcement for a Post-Doctoral Position in the Dependable Computing Systems Lab (DCSL)

Saurabh Bagchi

Posted on: September 14, 2013

 

Position Title: Post-doctoral Research Fellow

Position Summary: Researcher to provide leadership design and implementation in a project on dependable distributed applications. The work will involve designing and building usable tools for assessing vulnerabilities of software systems (e.g., OpenStack) and then designing protocols for automatically identifying root causes of vulnerabilities. The project involves 3 graduate students.

Project Summary: The project has funding from National Science Foundation and Northrop Grumman. It is targeted at building reliable distributed systems that can be relied on to do mission planning. Some parts of the distributed system will involve resource-constrained embedded devices. Specifically, we will have to develop tools for automating the highly specialized task of finding bugs and vulnerabilities in distributed software systems.

Type of Experience Required: PhD in Computer Science or Computer Engineering with strong systems building expertise, as evidenced through publications at top tier conferences. It is likely that a candidate with less than 5 publications at top tier conferences (DSN, NSDI, OSDI, SOSP, Usenix ATC, etc.) will not be competitive for this position.
